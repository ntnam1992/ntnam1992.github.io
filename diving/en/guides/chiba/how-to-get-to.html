Great for Day Trip from Tokyo

Chiba is one of the best destinations for a day trip from Tokyo. Even Ito, a popular dive site for shark diving in Chiba and one of the furthest from Tokyo, is only about 3 hours away by train (Model schedule below). There are 4 ways to get to Chiba: by train, bus, car, or taxi. Use whichever suits your trip the most. If you land in Narita International Airport in Chiba, it’s also possible to head directly to the dive site.

Diving Guide

A Day of Diving in Ito

Ito is a famous shark dive site in Japan, probably the most popular dive site in Chiba among divers from around the world. “Shark Tornado” created by hundreds of banded houndsharks is definitely worth seeing, not to mention meeting the friendly red stingray, bulgyhead wrasse, and longtooth grouper. There’s no place like it! The local dive shop ITO DIVING SERVICE BOMMIE is accustomed to having international guests frequently, and English communication is available. The water temperature ranges from 14 to 27℃

START

1
Head for JR Tateyama Station

About 150 min

JR Tateyama station is the closest you can get to Ito by train.

If you are heading to Tateyama station from Tokyo station, use rapid train (Sobu line) to Narita Airport which leaves at 6:10am (Platform 3). The convenien ce store in Tokyo station will not open until 6:30, so be sure to prepare your breakfast and lunch beforehand if needed!

You will arrive at JR Chiba station at 6:51am (Platform10).

Change trains to local train (Uchibo line) leaving JR Chiba station at 6:58am, and you will arrive at JR Tateyama station at 8:46am.

2
JR Tateyama Station to Ito

About 30 min

Staff of ITO DIVING SERVICE BOMMIE will pick you up at JR Tateyama station. The ride will take about 30 min.

3
Arrival

About 5 min

Get ready to dive! You will be leaving your belongings at the rest house.

Time to set your equipment and listen to the instructions at the harbor less than 5 minutes’ walk away from the dive shop.

4
10:00- 1st dive

The boat will leave at 10:00. Dive site “Okimae-ne” is not so far from the harbor; you will soon find yourself surrounded by sharks…! Enter the water by giant stride entry from the side of the boat and follow the rope down to the ocean floor, as instructed by the dive guide. You will be welcomed by banded houndsharks and red stingrays. Bulgyhead wrasse and longtooth grouper should be also nearby, perhaps examining the divers. Enjoy 40~50 min. dive, stop for safety at 5m, and exit the water.

*If you come to Ito during December to April, you may even be able to see Japanese cormorant (water bird) dive into the water to prey during safety stop!

There will be a ladder at the side of the boat, and the captain will help you get up.

5
Rest

Once you get back to the harbor, change your scuba tank, and leave your equipment there for the next dive. Go back to the dive shop, take a shower, and rest! There are free coffee bags, tea bags, and snacks at the rest house. Get ready for the next dive…

6
12:00- 2nd dive

About 5 min

7
After Diving

Wash your equipment, take a shower, change clothes… or have lunch first!

Most divers have lunch at the restaurant “Daibo”. It is right across the street from the dive shop, and you can enjoy delicious sea food. “Daibo” is open till 4:00pm. There are no convenience stores near buy, so if you prefer anything other than sea food, it is better to bring one with you.

8
Leave the Dive Shop and Head Back to Tokyo

The dive shop will take you back to Tateyama Station. You can get back to Tokyo the same way you’ve come, but be careful because there are not many trains! There are hot springs and places to have dinner in Tateyama, but most Japanese divers prefer to leave Tateyama before 4:00 PM. The last train to Tokyo leaves Tateyama at around 10:00 PM.

Or Stay the Night in Katsuura!

If you’re not in a rush to get back to Tokyo, you can venture out to Katsuura, approximately 80 minutes by train from Tateyama Station. There you will find the Katsuura Hotel Mikazuki, with a wonderful view of the southern Boso ocean. Stay here to enjoy an open-door hot spring with a view of the Pacific Ocean and a fresh seafood meal before settling in for a relaxing night’s sleep.

The next morning, before you go back to Tokyo, make sure to enjoy the diving at Katsuura as well! A boat dive will afford you astonishing views of the migratory fish, such as isaki and yellowtail, swimming around. Katsuura is also a good place to take an up-close look at some macro life such as seahorses and nudibranchs.
